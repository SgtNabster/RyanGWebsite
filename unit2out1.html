1.5 Describing copyright and other constraints affecting websites.

Copyright affects websites in multiple ways, a lot of pre-existing technologies have been copyrighted by one company or another so most new websites need to make sure they don't steal intellectual property by accident and ensure that the images / designs used are royalty-free or made from scratch.

Other constraints affecting websites involve[1];

  • Client constraints - Keeping to client constraints can be hard, straying to far from their company ethos could end in them rejecting the website
  • Project constraints - Budget, Deadlines etc.
  • User experience - Ensuring the website is pleasant to look at and viewable on multiple devices/operating systems.

1.6 Access issues that need taking into account.

One issue that can be fixed pretty easily is making sure your web page is screen-reader friendly for those with poor eyesight.
This involves making sure that your web page doesn't have too much clutter, is structured well and the image alt text is descriptive[2].

Ensuring your website has a tab through order is also high on the priority list[3]. Making sure the buttons on the page are defined enough to be seen and stand out is also required, along with ensuring no important information is shown as an image, as screen readers can't read that.
