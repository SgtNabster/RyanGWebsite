1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business.

The increase of IT in buisness has affected everything from customer service to online shopping.
It is also cheaper and easier to reach your target audience to advertise online than TV/Radio.
As computers speed up it also increases the speed at which businesses can complete complex computing tasks.

"Information technology fosters innovation in business. Innovation results in smarter apps, improved data storage, faster processing, and wider information distribution.
Innovation makes businesses run more efficiently. And innovation increases value, enhances quality, and boosts productivity."[1]

Overall using IT has very little disadvantage, providing the software is made to purpose and hardware is maintained correctly.

1.2 Methods, skills and resources needed to complete digital projects successfully.

Some methods for digital projects are things such as Agile, Kanban planning and good time management.
Also knowing what your project is aiming to achieve (what the problem is and how you will fix it for instance) is helpful.

Some other required skills are things like Attention to detail, HTML&CSS, problem solving[2] and some programming understanding dependant on the project.

Attention to detail can help you see if anything has been missed and that all angles have been looked at.
HTML&CSS are useful for digital advertising (most products have a website these days).
problem solving is always useful in a project, looking at what went wrong or what is not working and being able to fix it.

1.4 Risks that might impact digital projects.

The biggest risk in all digital project's is a failure to plan, this is why most projects use risk mitigation, which is used to plan for any risk that may arise in a project.

"By investing time upfront to address risks head-on, you’ll be better prepared to turn unexpected events into new opportunities, instead of spending your days putting out fires." [3]

Some reasons projects fail are[4]:

  • Overambition.
  • Fixed plans that don’t account for change.
  • Limited digital strategy.
